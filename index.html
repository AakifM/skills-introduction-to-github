What is water pollution?

water pollution, the release of substances into subsurface groundwater or into lakes, streams, rivers, estuaries, and oceans to the point that the substances interfere with beneficial use of the water or with the natural functioning of ecosystems. In addition to the release of substances, such as chemicals, trash, or microorganisms, water pollution may include the release of energy, in the form of radioactivity or heat, into bodies of water.

te parahanga o te wai, te tukunga o nga matū ki roto i te wai o raro o te whenua, ki roto ranei i nga roto, i nga awa, i nga awa, i nga wahapu, i nga moana, ka paopao nga matū ki te whakamahi pai o te wai, ki te mahi maori ranei o te rauwiringa kaiao. I tua atu i te tukunga o nga matū, penei i te matū, te paru, te moroiti ranei, ka uru pea te parahanga wai ki te tukunga o te kaha, i te ahua o te reo irirangi, te wera ranei, ki roto i nga tinana wai.

Why is Water Pollution Important?

Water pollution is a serious environmental issue that affects humans, animals, and ecosystems. It can lead to unsafe drinking water, harm to marine life, and damage to the environment.

He tino raru taiao te parahanga wai e pā ana ki te tangata, ngā kararehe me ngā pūnaha hauropi. Ka pā kino ki te wai inu, ka whakamate i ngā kararehe moana, ā, ka whakakino i te taiao.

Types of Water Pollution

There are different types of water pollution, including chemical pollution, plastic waste, oil spills, and sewage discharge. Each type affects water in harmful ways.

He maha ngā momo parahanga wai, pēnei i te parahanga matū, te para kirihou, te maringi hinu me te tukunga parakaingaki. Ka whakakino ēnei momo i te wai i ngā huarahi kino.

How Water Pollution Affects Us

Polluted water can cause health problems, affect food sources like fish, and reduce biodiversity in rivers and oceans.

Mā te wai kua parua e puta ai ngā raruraru hauora, e pā ai ki ngā kai pērā i te ika, ā, e heke ai te kanorau koiora i ngā awa me ngā moana.

Kaitiakitanga & Manaakitanga

Kaitiakitanga is a Māori concept of Guardianship.It means protecting and caring for the environment including land, water, air, and all living things. It reflects the belief that people are not owners of nature, but caretakers with a duty to look after it for future generations.

Ko te kaitiakitanga he ariā Māori mō te tiaki me te manaaki i te taiao — te whenua, te wai, te hau, me ngā mea ora katoa. Ehara te tangata i te rangatira o te taiao, engari he kaitiaki mō ngā uri whakatipu.

Manaakitanga is about showing respect, care, and kindness to others. It includes caring for people and the environment. In the context of water, it means treating our rivers and lakes with the same kindness and respect we give to people.

Ko te manaakitanga he tohu o te whakaute, te atawhai, me te aroha ki te tangata me te taiao. I te horopaki o te wai, ko te tikanga he tiaki, he whakanui i ō tātou awa me ngā roto pēnei i te whānau ake.
