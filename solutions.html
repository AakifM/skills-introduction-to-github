What You Can Do to Help Stop Water Pollution?

  • Proper Waste Disposal: Dispose of rubbish and chemicals properly instead of dumping them in drains or rivers.
    Te Whakaputu Para Tika: Whakaputuhia ngā para me ngā matū ki ngā wāhi tika, kaua e whiua ki ngā awa, ki ngā pūaha rānei.
  • Use Natural Fertilisers: Farmers can use compost and natural fertilisers to reduce chemical runoff.
    Whakamahia ngā Maniua Taiao: Ka taea e ngā kaiahuwhenua te whakamahi i te maniua taiao hei whakaiti i te rerenga matū.
  • Recycle and Reduce Plastic Use: Reduce single-use plastic and recycle items to prevent plastic pollution in oceans.
    Whakamahia Anō, Whakaitihia te Kirihou: Whakaitia te whakamahi kirihou kotahi anake, ā, whakamahia anō ngā mea hei ārai i te parahanga kirihou.
  • Plant Trees and Wetlands: Trees and wetlands filter dirty water before it enters rivers or lakes.
    Whakatō Rākau me ngā Repo: Ka tātari ngā rākau me ngā repo i te wai paru i mua i te urunga ki ngā awa, ki ngā roto rānei.
  • Pick up Rubbish: Join or start a local clean-up event to help keep water clean.
    Kohia te Para: Whakauruhia, ka timata ranei i tetahi huihuinga horoi o te rohe hei awhina i te wai kia ma
