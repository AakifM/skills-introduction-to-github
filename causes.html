Main Causes of Water Pollution

  • Industrial waste: Factories release chemicals into rivers and oceans.
    Parahanga ahumahi: Ka tukuna e ngā wheketere ngā matū ki ngā awa me ngā moana.
  • Agricultural runoff: Fertilisers and pesticides flow into waterways.
    Rere ā-ngā pāmu: Ka rere ngā maniua me ngā patu ngarara ki roto i te wai.
  • Plastic pollution: Rubbish like bottles and bags end up in the sea.
    Parahanga kirihou: Ka mutu ngā ipu me ngā pēke ki te moana.
  • Sewage and wastewater: Human and animal waste often gets into water bodies without proper treatment, spreading harmful bacteria and diseases.
    Parāwai me te wai para: He maha ngā wā ka rere te para tangata me te para kararehe ki roto i ngā wai, kāore i te tukatuka tika, ā, ka horapa ngā huakita me ngā mate kino.
  • Oil spills: Oil spills from ships and factories cause major damage to marine life and water quality.
    Ngā mātao hinu: Ka tino kino ngā mātao hinu mai i ngā kaipuke me ngā wheketere ki ngā kararehe moana me te kounga wai.
  • Atmospheric deposition: Pollutants from the air, like mercury and acid rain, settle into rivers and lakes.
    Te tukunga ā-rangi: Ka taka ngā parahanga i te rangi, pēnei i te mercury me te ua ācide, ki roto i ngā awa me ngā roto.
